General

Controlled Copies

Controlled copies of documented information are those that are maintained on an up-to-date basis ensuring that the user/reader always has the latest information on hand. Controlled copies of documented information shall always be updated in the event of a revision.

UnDocument/Data Recall

Whenever approved changes to documents/data are initiated as per the Change Management Procedure the QHSE Representative will raise the relevant document/data to the new issue number and will distribute copies in accordance with the Document Master List accordingly. The copies shall be accompanied by a Document/Process Change Notice Form giving instructions for the out of date copies to be destroyed or returned to the QHSE Representative. Action taken shall be demonstrated by the signature of the person completing the form. It shall be the responsibility of all users to ensure they are working to the correct document/data issues.

Forms

Forms are referred to in the procedures as appropriate. Making copies of individual forms is permitted for operational purposes as long as the copies have the same issue number. This will ensure that the content and format is consistent

Forms on computer will be permitted as long as the content and format is consistent and the issue number is up to date as per the original form held by the QHSE Representative or Designated Document Controller

Document Transmittal Sheets.

All documents/data with contractual importance sent out from the Company to clients, suppliers or employees will be accompanied by a Document Transmittal Sheet

Control of Computer Data

Back-up copies of all master files on servers will be produced and stored in a fireproof safe

Faxes

Faxes will be controlled through a logging in register located beside fax machines.

Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS).

Control of MSDS is maintained through registered manuals or registered CD ROM.Copies of manuals are assumed to be uncontrolled when individually distributed in accordance with this procedure. Making uncontrolled copies of individual MSDS is permitted only when used for reference purpose. Uncontrolled copies shall be taken only from controlled manuals, master documents or a controlled computer disk copy. Any copies printed off the computer must be assumed to be uncontrolled copies.

PREPARATION OF INTERNAL

Description of What the Procedures Will Contain

Procedures will usually comprise the following information in the order shown

Purpose

This section shall outline the objective or intention of the document.

Scope

Description stating by whom and how the work should be carried out. The procedure should be clear, concise and should be suited to the process requirement/objective. As uniform a style, as possible should be adopted, of which this document is an example. The preferred font is Times New Roman - 12 point.

Responsibilities

This section shall outline a person(s) who is responsible for ensuring that certain sections or the whole procedure is carried out in accordance with any stated requirements.

Cross References

This section shall list any associated documentation that will help the reader/user perform the task or function required.

Draft Issue Status

The draft issue status of all documents prepared will be indicated by showing “draft’ written in the issue field. Draft documents will be circulated to relevant persons for comment. Revised draft copies will be re-circulated with the revised sections/parts of the draft document highlighting the change.

Approval Issue Status

Once the responsible person and all relevant personnel to whom it has been circulated are satisfied with the content of the document the issue status will change from (indicating draft status) to a letter issue (indicating approved status) starting at A, B, etc. The Managing Director will initial the documents master list in each listed document to indicate that it has been approved.

Forms

Forms will be referred to as part of the relevant procedure to enable the reader/user to understand the QHSE procedures and have easy access to the required form. The control mechanism for forms is the form number and the issue status (number & date). The issue and control of procedures and forms content list will be kept updated by the QHSE Representative.

Document Information

All pages of a document will state the Page Number, Document Reference number and issue status. Additionally a unique revision number and date for every page is included as footer.

Numbering of Management System Documentation

At the preparation stage of a document the originator will liaise with the QHSE Representative and Designated Document Controller, who will in accordance with this procedure allocate the next sequential number for the document being prepared from the Document Register. This will avoid duplicate document numbers being issued into the system.

QHSE Documents will be numbered using a letter indicating the type of the document and a serial 3 digits number, for instance F 013 means form, P 004 means procedure, etc.

Supporting Documentation

These documents may include forms and flowcharts, technical information; sales and marketing information; Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS); Harmonized Offshore Chemical Notification Format (HOCNF) forms etc.

Document Changes

.Management System documentation can be split into two categories: draft documents and approved documents..

Draft Documents

Changes to the document will be implemented by the originator as appropriate in accordance with the comments received and forwarded to the QHSE Representative. The revised sections/parts of the draft document shall be indicated by italic bold font. The document will also be distributed for comments. The revised draft copies will be re-circulated to those personnel on the original distribution list.

Approved Documents

Any change to an approved document will be controlled by the QHSE Representative or delegate. A Change Request Note (CRN) will be raised by the QHSE Representative prior to issuing the document for review.

Copies of the document being changed may accompany the CRN for clarification.

The change request shall be approved by the QHSE Representative after the review has been completed.

Responsibility for approval shall lie with the managing director of the company or delegate and the QHSE Representative for the approval of procedural changes.

Review of change request(s) shall be required by those functions that performed the original review of the document, i.e. those positions stated on the approval document.

The QHSE Representative will distribute the change request to the relevant personnel to review the change. Following submission of the change request, the request will either be accepted or rejected.

Accepted

Following acceptance of the change request, the amendments will then be included into the original document being changed. The document number of the original document will then be amended, i.e. Issue 2 to 3. Obsolete pages, sections or the whole document will be clearly identified "SUPERCEDED" and will depend on the extent of the change. Superseded pages or sections will be filed by the QHSE Representative or Designated Document Controller in the "Superseded File". Change Request Notes (CRN's) will be filed by the QHSE Representative or Designated Document Controller. Issue and distribution of changes to documents will be in accordance with QHSE Management System requirements. Minor changes to pages/sections within a document will require amending the revision number on the page without need to amending the issue letter for the whole document. Changes will be signified by highlighting the change area in the same way as for draft documents, or by a red line in the right hand border.

Rejected

Following rejection of a request, after discussions with the originator when required, the CRN will be filed in the CRN file. The originator of the request will be notified of the rejection by the QHSE Representative and given a copy of the relevant CRN when completed.

Control of Latest Versions

Controlled copies of documents will be either distributed hard copies to users or being accessible through the company network on their computers in a DF format and not subject to changes. Red Stamped "CONTROLLED COPY No”. This will have a unique control copy number allocated to the document. This number will relate to the person or the department holding the copy and will be recorded in the Document Distribution Register.

Where the Documentation is accessible to all network linked personnel through their individual computers it is necessary to inform all relevant personnel concerned how to gain access to the computerized documents. Only the QHSE Representative is able to change the original documents. Changes to documents/data will be controlled through the Change Request Note system ensuring only the latest issue is on the computer system. Personnel within the Company or existing/potential customers requiring documents, for reference purposes only, will be issued a copy of the document/data demonstrating it is an uncontrolled copy. Documents of External Origin are those which externally generated such as international standards and specifications, customer related documents, etc will be dealt with same as company documents. All externally generated documents/data (as indicated above) received will be forwarded to the QHSE Representative for logging in the External Document Register and distributed as necessary.

Computer Data Back-Up Procedure

The central data base server in use shall be backed-up on a regular basis. The back-up tape will be changed by the authorized person or delegate and the current back-up tape will be stored in a fireproof cabinet. The monthly back-up tape will be stored for one year in the fireproof cabinet. A log detailing back-up tapes with allocated numbers will be stored in a safe area. The log can be checked at any time for the status of the back-ups.
